About Me

I am a licensed clinical social worker with over 15 years of experience helping individuals overcome anxiety, depression, and trauma-related challenges. My approach is warm, empathetic, and collaborative—I believe therapy works best when we work together as a team.

I became a therapist because I witnessed firsthand how mental health struggles can impact every aspect of a person's life. I've seen the incredible transformation that can happen when someone receives the right support and tools. My goal is to help you understand yourself better, develop healthy coping strategies, and create meaningful change in your life.

In my practice, I've worked with individuals from diverse backgrounds facing a wide range of challenges. I'm particularly passionate about helping people heal from trauma and develop resilience. I believe that everyone has the capacity for growth and healing, and I'm honored to be part of that journey.

Treatment Approach

Therapeutic Modalities

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): I use CBT to help clients identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors that contribute to their distress.
  • EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing): I'm certified in EMDR, which is highly effective for processing trauma and reducing its emotional impact.
  • Mindfulness-Based Therapy: I incorporate mindfulness practices to help clients stay present, reduce anxiety, and develop greater self-awareness.
  • Trauma-Informed Care: All of my work is grounded in understanding how trauma affects the brain and body, creating a safe space for healing.
  • Solution-Focused Therapy: I help clients identify their strengths and build on what's already working in their lives.

Philosophy of Care

I believe that therapy is a collaborative process. You are the expert on your own life, and my role is to provide support, guidance, and evidence-based tools to help you achieve your goals. I create a non-judgmental, safe space where you can explore your thoughts and feelings openly.

I take a holistic approach, considering not just your symptoms, but your entire life context—your relationships, work, physical health, and personal values. I believe in meeting you where you are and honoring your pace of change.
